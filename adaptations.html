Notable Adaptations

Pride & Prejudice (1967)

Pride & Prejudice was released as a miniseries in 1967 on BBC for the 150th anniversary of Jane Austen's death. This miniseries is known to be the earliest BBC TV adaptation, due to the earlier BBC adaptations from 1938 and 1952 being "lost". The middle sister, Mary, is excluded from this adaptation.

Pride & Prejudice (1995)

Another of BBC's Pride and Prejudice adaptations released as a miniseries as well. It remains true to the novel.

Bride and Prejudice (2004)

This Bollywood take on the classic by transporting the characters to modern-day settings complete with vibrant dance numbers that are unique to Bollywood. Starring the Iconic Aishwarya Rai as Lalita Bakshi, (Elizabeth Bennet) this film beautifully blends the two cultures.

Pride and Prejudice (2005)

This 2005 adaptation stands out for its captivating cinematography and soundtrack, Though it takes on its own liberties with the story and dialogue, it has left an impact on the younger generations, introducing them to the timeless works of Jane Austen.

Pride and Prejudice and Zombies

The Pride and Prejudice and Zombies movie is an adaptation of an adaptation. The novel written by Seth Grahame-Smith credits Austen as a co-author as he modern zombie fiction elements to this classic novel.
