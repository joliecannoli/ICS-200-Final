Elizabeth Bennet

The second daughter of Mr. and Mrs. Bennet, Elizabeth is the heroine and her father's favorite as they share similar opinions on society's conventions and the importance of wealth and status. Elizabeth is the most intelligent of her sisters as she is witty with a tongue that others percieve as too sharp for her own good. She is one of the most well known female characters in English literature.

Fitzwilliam Darcy

Mr. Darcy is the nephew of Lady Catherine de Bourgh. Though he is judgmental towards those that are of the lower classes, he is a shy gentleman which makes him come across as cold and prideful. Like Elizabeth, he also tends to judge harshly and hastily with his wealth playing a role in his excessive pride. Elizabeth's rejections to his proposals builds humility in Darcy as he continues to demonstrate his devotion to her.

Jane Bennet

Jane is the eldest sister who is considered to be the fairest by her mother. She is gentle and reserved, her pleasantness in her interactions with Charles Bingley contrasts with the mutual distase between Elizabeth and Darcy

Charles Bingley

Bingley is Darcy's best friend. His easygoing nature contrasts with Darcy's initially prideful and haughty demeanor as he is uncaring about differneces due to social class.
